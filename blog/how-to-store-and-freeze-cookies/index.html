How to Store and Freeze Cookies

Handmade, preservative-free cookies do not last forever, but this keeps them at their best.

Home › Blog › How to Store and Freeze Cookies

By Brydee, founder & baker · 2026-07-07

When cookies are baked fresh with no preservatives, they taste incredible, but they will not sit in the pantry for months like the supermarket kind. That is the honest trade-off for real ingredients. Here is how to keep them at their best, and the freezer trick that makes them last for weeks.

Storing on the bench

Keep your cookies in an airtight container at room temperature, out of direct sun. That is it. Do not refrigerate them, the fridge actually dries baked goods out faster. Stored this way, they are at their best for the first few days.

If you like a soft cookie, pop a small piece of bread in the container. The cookies draw moisture from it and stay soft. If you prefer them crisp, leave the bread out.

Freezing (the trick that makes them last)

Cookies freeze beautifully. To do it well:

  • Let them cool completely first.
  • Lay them in a single layer so they do not stick together, then transfer to an airtight container or freezer bag with baking paper between layers.
  • Freeze for up to about three months.

To enjoy, take out what you want and let them come to room temperature for fifteen to twenty minutes. For that just-baked feel, warm them in the oven at 150C for a few minutes. Honestly, warm-from-frozen might be even better than fresh.

A note on our cookies

Because our gluten-free, dairy-free cookies have no preservatives, we always suggest enjoying them within a few days or freezing them if you want to pace yourself. Order a bigger box, freeze half, and you have a proper treat on hand whenever you need one.

Frequently asked

Can you freeze gluten-free cookies?
Yes, gluten-free cookies freeze very well. Cool them fully, freeze in a single layer, then store airtight for up to three months.
How long do preservative-free cookies last?
At their best for a few days at room temperature in an airtight container, or up to about three months in the freezer.
