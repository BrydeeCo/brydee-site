Are Biscuits Ever Healthy?

An honest answer from someone who bakes them for a living.

Home › Blog › Are Biscuits Ever Healthy?

By Brydee, founder & baker · 2026-07-05

Let us be honest with each other: a biscuit is a treat, not a health food, and anyone telling you their biscuit is a superfood is selling something. But that does not mean all biscuits are equal. Some are genuinely better for you than others, and the difference comes down to a few simple things.

What makes a biscuit better for you

  • Lower sugar. Many biscuits are shockingly high in sugar. Gently sweetened ones let you enjoy the flavour without the spike.
  • Real, short ingredient lists. If you cannot pronounce half the ingredients, that is a flag. Real butter or oil, flour, a natural sweetener and flavour beats a list of numbers.
  • No preservatives. Freshly baked biscuits with no preservatives are closer to what you would make at home. They do not last as long, which is the honest trade-off for not being full of additives.
  • Portion. A couple of biscuits with a cup of tea is a treat. The whole packet in one sitting is a different story, and that is true of any biscuit.

What about gluten-free biscuits?

Gluten-free does not automatically mean healthier. In fact some shop-bought gluten-free biscuits pack in extra sugar and fillers to make up for the texture. The healthier gluten-free option is one that keeps the ingredient list real and the sugar low, rather than leaning on additives.

Our approach

We bake our gluten-free biscuits low in sugar, with real ingredients and no preservatives, because that is how we want to feed our own family. They are a treat, and we are not going to pretend otherwise, but they are a treat you can feel genuinely good about. They are dairy-free too.

Frequently asked

Are gluten-free biscuits healthier?
Not automatically. Some have extra sugar and fillers. A healthier one keeps the ingredients real and the sugar low, gluten-free or not.
What should I look for in a healthier biscuit?
Lower sugar, a short and recognisable ingredient list, no preservatives, and sensible portions.
