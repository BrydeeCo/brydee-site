20 Gluten-Free Snack Ideas (Sweet & Savoury)

Being gluten-free does not mean living on rice cakes. Here are twenty snacks we actually reach for.

Home › Blog › 20 Gluten-Free Snack Ideas (Sweet & Savoury)

By Brydee, founder & baker · 2026-07-01

When you first go gluten-free, snacks are where it gets tricky. So much of the grab-and-go stuff, crackers, muesli bars, wraps, pastries, is off the table. The good news is there are plenty of genuinely good options once you know where to look. Here are twenty we come back to, split into fresh, savoury and sweet.

Fresh and simple

  • Fresh fruit with a handful of nuts
  • Veggie sticks with hummus or guacamole
  • Greek or coconut yoghurt with berries
  • Apple slices with peanut butter
  • A boiled egg and cherry tomatoes

Savoury

  • Rice cakes or corn thins with avocado
  • Cheese (naturally gluten-free) with GF crackers
  • Roasted chickpeas
  • Popcorn (plain, air-popped)
  • Nut and seed mix
  • Olives and marinated veg
  • Tinned tuna or salmon on cucumber rounds

Sweet

  • Dark chocolate (check it is gluten-free)
  • Medjool dates stuffed with nut butter
  • A smoothie with banana and cacao
  • Chia pudding
  • Homemade bliss balls
  • Gluten-free biscuits or cookies (yes, ours) with a cup of tea

What to watch for on labels

Gluten hides in places you would not expect: soy sauce, some flavoured chips, muesli bars, and anything with malt, wheat starch or barley. In Australia, look for products clearly labelled gluten-free, and if you have coeliac disease, be extra careful with anything made in a shared facility, where cross-contamination is a real risk.

Frequently asked

Are all these snacks safe for coeliacs?
Most are naturally gluten-free, but always check packaged items for a gluten-free label and watch for cross-contamination if you have coeliac disease.
What is a good sweet gluten-free snack?
Dark chocolate, dates with nut butter, or a couple of properly made gluten-free biscuits are all easy wins.
